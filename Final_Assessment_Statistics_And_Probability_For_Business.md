Assessment Overview
This assessment requires you to collaboratively work in teams to design and conduct a research project that identifies and addresses a challenge within the Kepler Community using statistical analysis. It will equip you with practical skills in data collection, analysis and interpretation, and data-informed decision-making. In your respective teams, you will begin by selecting a relevant issue affecting the Kepler Community in any area of your choice such as academic performance, student engagement, digital skills, resource utilization, etc. Then, you will apply descriptive statistics to summarize and visualize the collected data, identifying key trends and insights. Thereafter, you will use inferential statistics including hypothesis testing to evaluate relationships or differences within the data and draw meaningful conclusions. The results will inform evidence-based recommendations to address the identified challenge. Your work will be assessed on your ability to
-	GCS12 Estimate the uncertainty associated with statistical parameter estimation
-	ARW10 Work with a team to interpret data and make data-informed decisions.

Directions
Follow the step-by-step instructions below to complete this research project
1.	Identify a Community Challenge

○	Choose a relevant and measurable issue affecting the Kepler community (e.g., student success, resource accessibility, attendance, digital skill development, etc.).
○	Clearly define your research question or problem statement. Use data where applicable to argue why the challenge needs immediate attention. 
○	Formulate your null hypothesis (H0) and alternative hypothesis (H1) which will guide your study. 

2.	Design Your Research

○	Determine what data/variables you need to collect (quantitative, qualitative, or both) using specific examples. Justify your choice. 
○	Plan your data collection method(s): primary (survey, observation, interviews, focus groups, etc.), secondary research (existing data), or both. Be sure to justify your choice. Create the corresponding data collection tools. 
○	Ensure ethical and accurate data handling: Request approval to conduct research from Kepler Community. Send the email to 	research@keplercollege.ac.rw and copy your instructor(s). 

3.	Process Your Data: After collecting data, clean the data and be sure to keep both original (raw) data and clean data in separate tabs. 
4.	Analyze Your Data
○	Use descriptive statistics (e.g., mean, median, mode, standard deviation, charts, graphs, etc.) to summarize your data and identify trends.
○	Compute relevant hypothesis testing as part of inferential statistics to test assumptions and draw conclusions. 

5.	Interpret and Discuss Findings

○	Explain what your results mean in relation to the identified challenge.
○	Discuss any limitations of your data or analysis.
○	Suggest realistic, data-informed actions or recommendations for improvement within the Kepler community in relation to addressing the identified challenge 

6.	Prepare Your Final Deliverables

○	Written Report (approx. 1,500–2,000 words) 1.5 line spacing, 12 Font Size, Times New Roman covering the following sections:
i.	Introduction
1.	Discuss your chosen challenge within the Kepler community using that where needed to clarify why it was important to be researched on.
2.	Specify what was your objective of the research 
3.	State your H0 and H1 that guided your research 
ii.	Methodology
1.	Discuss the collected variables and specify their sub-types. Be sure to discuss why these variables were relevant to your research objective. 
2.	Discuss what was your population and sample size, including sampling strategies used to select the sample from the population. 
3.	Discuss whether you conducted primary, secondary, or both research and why, including the data collection tool you used. 
iii.	Data Processing: Discuss how you cleaned data by specifying the data quality issues identified in the raw data and how you fixed them. 
iv.	Data Analysis
1.	Include relevant tables, graphs, and/or charts used to visualize the findings.
2.	Include relevant measures of central tendency and spread used to analyze the data and identify trends. Note that these should base on whether your data is approximately normally distributed or skewed. 
3.	Talk about the appropriate statistical test used to test your H0 
4.	Present the results of your statistical tests to identify the test statistic and corresponding p-value. Be sure to set corresponding critical value and significance level as threshold of decision making of whether to reject or fail to reject the null hypothesis. 
v.	Conclusion and Recommendations: Based on the results of your analysis above, summarize your key findings (what the analysis tells you about your research objective and hypothesis) and provide relevant recommendations of what should be done to improve your chosen area/challenge to improve the wellbeing within Kepler Community. 
vi.	Appendices: Attach various supporting documents such as (raw data and clean data, email approval to conduct research within Kepler Community (from directorate of research). 
vii.	 (form responses, etc.)
○	Slide Presentation (7 - 10 slides): Summarize your report sections above in 7 - 10 slides and be ready to present your work in 10 - 12 min per group.
