1. Introduction (1 – 1.5 pages) 
This section introduces the research project by explaining the specific challenge affecting the 
Kepler community, such as low student engagement, attendance issues, or limited digital skills. It 
should clearly describe why this issue is important and why it needs immediate attention, supported 
by brief evidence or observations. The section must also state the main objective of the study and 
present a clear research question or problem statement. Finally, include the null hypothesis (H₀), 
which assumes no relationship or effect, and the alternative hypothesis (H₁), which suggests that a 
relationship or effect exists. 
2. Methodology (2.5 – 3 pages) 
This section explains how the research was conducted. It should describe the variables used in the 
study, including their types (quantitative or qualitative) and why they are relevant to the research 
objective. The population and sample size must be clearly defined, along with the sampling method 
used to select participants. Additionally, explain whether the research used primary data (such as 
surveys or interviews), secondary data, or both, and justify this choice. The section should also 
describe the data collection tools used and include a brief explanation of ethical considerations, 
such as obtaining permission and ensuring confidentiality. 
3. Data Processing (0.5 – 1 page) 
This section describes how the collected data was prepared for analysis. It should explain the 
condition of the raw data and identify any data quality issues such as missing values, duplicates, 
or errors. Then, clearly describe the steps taken to clean the data, including how errors were 
corrected and inconsistencies resolved. It is also important to mention that both the original (raw) 
data and the cleaned data were kept separately for transparency and accuracy. 
4. Data Analysis (3 – 4 pages) 
This section presents and analyzes the data using statistical methods. It should begin with 
descriptive statistics, including measures such as mean, median, mode, and standard deviation, to 
summarize the data. Tables, charts, and graphs should be included to visually present trends and 
patterns. The section should also describe whether the data is normally distributed or skewed. 
Furthermore, inferential statistics must be applied by explaining the statistical test used (such as a 
t-test or chi-square test), presenting the test statistic and p-value, and stating the chosen 
significance level (usually 0.05). Based on these results, clearly indicate whether the null 
hypothesis is rejected or not. 
5. Interpretation and Discussion (2.5 – 3 pages) 
This section explains the meaning of the results obtained from the data analysis. It should connect 
the findings back to the research question and describe what the results reveal about the identified 
challenge. Any patterns or trends observed should be discussed in detail. Additionally, this section 
should acknowledge the limitations of the study, such as a small sample size, possible bias, or data 
collection challenges, and explain how these limitations may affect the results. 
6. Conclusion and Recommendations (2 page) 
This section summarizes the key findings of the research and provides a clear conclusion based on 
the analysis and hypothesis testing. It should restate what the results indicate about the research 
objective and whether the hypothesis was supported or not. The section must also include practical 
and realistic recommendations that can help address the identified challenge within the Kepler 
community. These recommendations should be directly based on the data findings.
